## Template 1 — CAD Logs & Dispatch Audio (RCW 42.56)

```
[Date]

[Name]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]

VIA EMAIL: [agency public records officer email]

RE: PUBLIC RECORDS REQUEST — [Agency Name]
Request No.: [leave blank for agency to assign]

To Whom It May Concern:

Pursuant to the Washington Public Records Act (RCW 42.56), I request the
following records related to incident [Case Number / Incident Date / Location]:

1. Complete Computer-Aided Dispatch (CAD) logs for [Incident Date]
   between [Time Range, e.g., 11:00 AM – 7:00 PM PDT], for the following
   address/location: [Location / Zone]

2. All dispatch audio recordings (including 911 calls, radio traffic, and
   non-emergency lines) for the same time period and location.

3. Names, badge/employee IDs, and certification numbers of all dispatchers,
   call-takers, and supervisory personnel who handled any call related to
   this incident.

4. Any and all internal notes, memos, emails, or reports related to this
   incident, including quality assurance reviews and supervisory reviews.

5. If any portion of these records has been lost, destroyed, or is otherwise
   unavailable, please provide:
   a) A written explanation of when and how the loss occurred
   b) The name and title of the person(s) who last accessed each record
   c) Any chain-of-custody logs or audit trails for the records

I request these records in electronic format (CSV for CAD logs, MP3/WAV for
audio) via email or secure download link.

RCW 42.56.520 requires a response within five business days. If you determine
that any portion of this request exceeds the scope of disclosure or is exempt
from disclosure under a specific exemption, please identify the exemption and
the specific records withheld.

If there will be any charge for copying or production, please notify me in
advance. I am willing to pay reasonable costs up to $[Amount].

Thank you,

[Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## Template 2 — Personnel Records (RCW 42.56)

```
[Date]

[Name]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]

VIA EMAIL: [HR / Personnel records officer email]

RE: PUBLIC RECORDS REQUEST — Personnel File of [Employee Name]
Employment ID: [if known]

To Whom It May Concern:

Pursuant to the Washington Public Records Act (RCW 42.56), I request the
following records for [Employee Full Name], who was employed by [Agency Name]
as [Title] during the period [Start Date] through [End Date or Present]:

1. Complete personnel file, including:
   - Employment application and resume
   - All job descriptions held during employment
   - Performance evaluations
   - Disciplinary records (written reprimands, suspensions, terminations)
   - Training records and certifications (including date of hire, POST/EMT
     certs, continuing education)
   - Leave records for [Incident Date +/- 7 days]
   - Any complaints (internal or external) involving [Employee Name]
   - Reason for separation if employment ended

2. Any records related to certification or licensing held by [Employee Name]
   during the course of employment, including:
   - EMT certification (level, cert number, expiration)
   - POST / law enforcement certification
   - Dispatch certification (APCO, NENA, or equivalent)
   - Any other professional or occupational license

3. Any and all records, reports, or correspondence related to the incident
   on [Incident Date] involving [Employee Name] in [Location / Jurisdiction].

If you deny any portion of this request, please cite the specific exemption
authorizing the denial.

Thank you,

[Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## Template 4 — Agency Body-Worn / Dashcam Video (RCW 42.56.240)

```
[Date]

[Name]

VIA EMAIL: [Agency records officer]

RE: PUBLIC RECORDS REQUEST — Body-Worn Camera / Dashcam / In-Car Video
Incident: [Case Number / Incident Date / Location]

To Whom It May Concern:

Pursuant to the Washington Public Records Act (RCW 42.56) and RCW 42.56.240
(disclosure of body camera footage), I request:

1. All body-worn camera footage from any officer who responded to the incident
   at [Location] on [Date], between [Start Time] and [End Time].

2. All dashcam / in-car video from any law enforcement vehicle at the scene.

3. Any CCTV, surveillance video, or other recording of the incident from
   [Location] or surrounding area.

4. Any video or audio recorded by [Employee / Subject Name] during the incident.

5. Metadata for all recordings: file hash, creation timestamp, device ID,
   chain of custody.

I request these records in their original format without compression or
transcoding. If any portion has been redacted, please provide the unredacted
version for in-camera review.

Thank you,

[Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## Tips for Pro Se Filers

- **Deadline:** WA agencies must respond within 5 business days (RCW 42.56.520)
- **Fees:** First hour of staff time is free. After that, notify you before charging
- **Denials:** Must cite specific exemption. Do not accept blanket denials
- **Format:** Request electronic format — no paper, no CD-ROM
- **Breadth:** Start broad, narrow on follow-up. Easier to over-request and have them narrow than to miss something in the initial request
- **Tracking:** Use templates above with modification dates. Submit via email with read receipt
- **Dispute:** If denied, you can file a complaint with the WA Attorney General's Office or seek judicial review under RCW 42.56.550
